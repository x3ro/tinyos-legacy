TinyHop is a lightweight reactive routing protocol designed to work in Wireless Sensor Networks.

It is implemented in TinyOS version 2.0.2.
It has been designed to:
  • Reduce the power consumption by avoiding the proactive approach.
  • Work in constrained devices like the micaZ (4Kb Ram).
  • Control reliability with end-to-end acknowledgements.
  • Provide bidirectional communication in the transmission path.
  • Be easily configured with your own routing decision metrics.
  • Be simple, with a small footprint depending on the routing table.
  • Scale at the cost of keeping only fresh routes in the routing table.
    • TinyHop has been tested:
    1. In TOSSIM (up to 128 nodes with different gain level topologies).
    2. In a Real Tesbed (up to 32 micaZ nodes in a gym).

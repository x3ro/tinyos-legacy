Ad Hoc Hierarchical Routing in Low-Power Wireless Networks

by Konrad Iwanicki

 

This project contains a TinyOS 2.0 framework for ad hoc hierarchical point-to-point routing for wireless sensor networks. It was developed by Konrad Iwanicki in 2007-2009 and was presented at the Eighth ACM/IEEE International Conference on Information Processing in Sensor Networks (IPSN 2009), held in April 2009, in San Francisco, California, USA.

To develop the framework, the author has analyzed a number of ad hoc hierarchical routing protocols from major networking conferences and journals, focusing on the ones suitable for implementation on resource-constrained wireless devices. Of course the choice of the protocols is by no means meant to cover all the work on hierarchical routing. In particular, the sources available for download encompass three cluster hierarchy maintenance techniques presented in the IPSN'09 paper. The framework has been evaluated in TOSSIM, a TinyOS simulator, and on a 60-node testbed at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. The evaluation can be found in the IPSN'09 paper as well.

There is also a demo application and a Java front-end with a cool user interface, but they have not been made public yet. Maybe soon. Sorry...

The networked sensor regime is an exciting new design space that is emerging as a result of innovations in RF Communication technology and MEMS technology. TinyOS explores the software support that is required in that design space. TinyOS is a component-based runtime environment designed to provide support for deeply embedded systems which require concurrency intensive operations while constrained by minimal hardware resources. For example, originally designed for the Smart Dust hardware platform, our scheduler fits in under 200 bytes of program memory. 

For a full analysis and description of the TinyOS system, its component model, and its implications for networked sensor architectures read Architectural directions for Networked Sensors. (Submitted for publication 4/00) Additionally, a high level overview of TinyOS concepts can be found at TOSposter.ppt.

A talk given at the Endeavor Retreat (6/00) covering TinyOS and Tiny Networked Devices can be found at TinyOSTalk.ppt. Two posters were also presented at the retreat poster session. One was covering the basics of TinyOS and one covered the Active Message based messaging layer.

For more detailed information about TinyOS, you can also go: details.html

TinyOS is part of the WebS Project, and executed in collaboration with the Intel Research Lab at Berkeley.
